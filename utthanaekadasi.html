Utthanaāna Ekādaśī in the Month of Kārttika

In ancient times there was a demon named Śaṅka who was the son of Sāgara or the ocean. He forcefully took control of all the planets, including Indraloka. All the demigods had fled their planets and were hiding in a cave due to fear of this demon. The demon Śaṅkha thought, "Even though I have conquered all demigods, they still appear very powerful." He realised that the demigods derived their power from Vedic mantras, and thought, "I will plunder the Vedas so that all these demigods will become weak." With this consciousness, the demon Śaṅkha plundered all the Vedas from Satyaloka, the planet of Brahmā. While being taken by this demon, the personified Vedas, being afraid, ran away, escaping his control, and merged into the water along with yajña, mantra and all its seeds. The demon Śaṅkha entered into the ocean in search of the Vedas, but could not find them anywhere.

During this time the Lord, who was sleeping, was approached by the demigods offering Him prayers. Upon hearing their prayers, the Lord awoke and said, "O, demigods! I, being pleased by your prayers, want to give you a boon. You have woken Me on the Ekādaśī tithi of śukla-pakṣa (waxing moon) in the month of Kārttika. For this reason this tittha is very pleasant and respected by Me. The Vedas which were stolen by the demon Śaṅkha have been merged into the ocean. I will kill this demon Śaṅkha and bring you the Vedas. During this month of Kārttika, anyone who takes an early morning bath should be considered as having taken bath in avabhṛtha (the sanctified water) of the yajña. From today, I will reside in the water during the month of Kārttika. All you demigods along with the munis, please come with me into the water." Having said this, the Lord accepted the form of a fish and entered into the water from the sky. After killing the demon, Śaṅkha, the Lord came to the forest of Badarī or Badarikāśrama in the Himālayas. There He ordered all the ṛṣis, "Munīśvaras! All of you should immediately enter into the ocean and collect all the vedic mantras from the water. In the mean time I, along with all the demigods, will stay in Prayāga."

After receiving this order from the Lord all the mahārṣṣis, by dint of their austerity, rescued the Vedic mantras along with all yajñas and other mantras. From that day, the ṛṣis became connected to the particular mantras that they rescued and each became known as the ṛṣi of that mantra.

Then all the ṛṣis went together to Prayāga to meet the Lord. There they offered all the Vedic mantras to Lord Viṣṛu and Lord Brahmā. Brahmā was very happy to retrieve all the Vedas and he, along with all the devas and ṛṣis, performed an aśvamedha-yajña at Prayāga.

Taken from Bhakti Purusottama Swami's book, Damodara Karttika Vrata
