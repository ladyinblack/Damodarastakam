The Glories of Offering an Tulasī-pūjā in the Month of Kārttika

This is an ancient incident, involving two brāhmaņas from the state of Kāśmīra, Sumedha and Harimedha. They were both great Viṣṇu-bhaktas and always engaged in devotional service to Lord Viṣṇu. They were also very merciful towards all living entities and understood all truths properly. At one time, these two brāhmaṇas started their journey of pilgrimage, and eventually came across a garden of tulasī plants. At this point they were exhausted from the day's walking, but immediately upon seeing tulasī, Sumedha circumambulated the garden and offered his obeisances very respectfully. Seeing this, Harimedha asked Sumedha about the glories of tulasī and what the result of offering praṇāma to tulasī was. Sumedha said, "It is too hot here due to the direct sunshine, let us go take shelter of that banyan tree. There, in the shade of the banyan tree, I will tell you everything."

Sumedha then began, "Long, long ago, Indra, the king of heaven, had lost his position due to the curse of Durvāsā Muni. At that time Brahmā and all the demigods, as well as all the demons, were engaged in churning the milk ocean. From that churning of the milk ocean, many wonderful beings and items emerged, such as the elephant Airāvata, the Kalpa-vṛkṣa tree, Candramā or the moon, Lakṣmī, the horse Uccaiḥśravā and the Kaustugha gem. Lord Hari in the form of Dhanvantari also appeared, along with all celestial medicines and amṛta to nectar. As Lord Viṣṇu was holding that pot of nectar, which has the power to make one immortal, He felt great happiness. Due to feeling such pleasure, tears began to fall from His eyes and some tears fell into the pot of nectar.

The very moment this happened, tulasī appeared from the pot. All the demigods along with Brahmā offered tulasī and Lakṣmī to Lord Śrī Hari, which He accepted with great pleasure. Since then tulasī has been very dear to Lord Viṣṇu, who is the protector of the entire universe. tulasī is His dearmost, therefore I offered her my obeisances." As Sumedha was giving the explanation they saw an effulgent chariot racing across the sky towards them.

Just then the banyan tree under which they were sitting was uprooted and two celestial beings appeared from within it. They shone as bright as the sun and lit all the directions. They offered their obeisances to Sumedha and Harimedha, who were surprised and quite overwhelmed. They asked, "Who are you both? Your bodies appear very auspicious, like the devatās." Hearing this, the two effulgent personalities spoke, "Oh, great brāhmaṇas! You are our father, mother, guru, friend and everything to us." One of them said, "My name is Astika, I am a resident of Devaloka. One day I went to Nandana-kānana to play, where I engaged in pleasurable activities with many celestial ladies for as long as they desired. At that time the necklace of pearls and belī flowers from one of the ladies, fell on Lomaśa Ṛṣi who was engaged in tapasya at that place. Upon seeing this, Lomaśa Muni became very angry and he wanted to curse her. But after consideration he thought, "Ladies have no independence, they always depend on others, therefore this wicked Astika should be punished." He then cursed me, saying, "You shall become a brahma- rākṣasa and reside in a banyan tree." After hearing this curse placed upon me, I approached the muni very humbly and asked him, "O, great sage! Please tell me how long I will remain as a brahma-rākṣasa and how can I be delivered from this curse?" Being pleased with my humility, the muni said, "When you hear the holy name of Lord Viṣṇu and the glories of tulasī from the mouth of a brāhmaṇa then you will immediately be released from this curse and be liberated." Since then I havebeen residing in this banyan tree as a brahma-rākṣasa in a miserable condition. Today, having had darśana of both of you, I have been delivered from the curse of Lomaśa Muni.

"Now please hear about my friend. He was a great muni and always engaged in the service of his guru. Once he disobeyed the order of his most respectable guru and thus became a brahma-rākṣasa. Today by your mercy he has also been delivered."

Having told their stories, both of them offered obeisances to the two brāhmaṇas, circumambulated them and then left fro the divya-dhāma. The two great brāhmaṇas, reciting the glories of tulasī, then left that place and proceeded on their tīrtha-yātrā. This illustrates the importance of worshipping tulasī in the month of Kārttika, which gives immense pleasure to Lord Viṣṇu.

Taken from Bhakti Purusottama Swami's book, Damodara Karttika Vrata
