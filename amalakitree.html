Importance of the Āmalakī Tree in the Month of Kārttika

It has been stated in the scriptures that during the month of Kārttika, on the Caturdaśī during the śukla-pakṣa, one should worship the āmalakī tree, as this great tree removes all sins. This Caturdaśī is known as Vaikuṇṭha-caturdaśī, and it is recommended that on this day one should worship Śrī Hari along with Śrī Rādhā under the shadow of an āmalakī tree. One should then circumambulate the three one hundred and eight times and offer obeisances and prayers to Lord Śy&257;masundara, Śrī Kṛṣṇa. After offering prayers, one should sit down under the āmalakī tree and listen to the glories of āmalakī. Finally, one should feed some brāhmaṇas and offer them dakṣiṇā, as pleasing the brāhmaīas gives pleasure to Lord Śrī Hari, who is the giver of liberation.
Appearance of the āmalakī tree:

At the beginning of creation, the entire universe was submerged under water. At that time Lord Brahmā began chanting the holy name of the Lord. Out of ecstasy due to eagerness to see the Lord, tears fell from Brahmā's eyes. That water which consisted of his tears of love fell on the earth planet. From these tears, the āmalakī tree appeared and developed into a huge tree, expanding its branches and sub-branches, and was laden with āmalakī fruits. The āmalakī tree appeared first, before any other tree, and is therefore known as "ādiruha". "Ādi" means original and "ruha" means tree. After the creation of the āmalakī tree, all other living entities were created. When the devas were created they all came to the place where Lord Viṣṇu's favourite tree, the āmalakī, was born. They were surprised to see the āmalakī tree. Just then, a voice fromt he sky proclaimed to the demigods. "This āmalakī tree is the best among all trees, because it is very dear to Lord Viṣṇu. Just by remembering this tree one will achieve the result of giving a cow in charity. By taking darśana of this tree one will double this result. By eating the fruit of this tree one will triple this result. One should always serve the āmalakī tree very carefully, because this tree is very dear to Lord Viṣṇu and has the power to destroy all sins. One should worship the āmalakī tree for the fulfilment of all one's desires. Whoever worships Lord Śrī Hari in forests of āmalakī during the month of Kārttika, and takes prasāda under the shadow of this tree, will be free from all kinds of sins. Any pious activities performed under the shadow of the āmalakī tree will give millions of times the benefit.

Long, long ago there was a great brāhmaṇa named Devaśarmā, who was living on the northern bank of the Kāverī River. He was very expert in all the Vedas and Vedāṅgas. Unfortunately he had a son who was a great durācārī, a notorious person devoid of any good behaviour. One day Devaśarmā, for the benefit of his son, advised him, "O, my dear son! Now it is the month of Kārttika which is very dear to Lord Viṣṇu. You should follow all the rules, take an early bath daily, give charity, and follow a vrata during this month. You should worship Lord Viṣṇu with tulasī mañjarīs. Offer a lamp to the Lord, offer obeisances and circumambulate Lord Śrī Hari." When that wicked boy heard this advice from his father he became very angry. His lips began to tremble in anger and he rebuked and blasphemed his father, saying, "Father! I do not need the puṇya of performing a Kārttika-vrata. I will not observe this Kārttika-vrata." After hearing this kind of loose talk from his son, Devaśarmā with great anger said, "O less intelligent! I curse you to become a rat and reside in the trunk of a tree."

Upon hearing this curse from his father, the boy became very afraid. He offered his obeisance to his father and asked, "Please tell me how I can become free from that species of life? How long will I remain in the form of a rat?" After hearing this gentle speech of his son, Devaśarmā was pleased and said, "When you hear the glories of Kārttika-vrata, which is very dear to Lord Viṣṇu, you will be liberated." After hearing this from his father he immediately transformed into the form of a rat and stayed in a dense forest for many years. One day, in the month of Kārttika, Viśvāmitra Ṛṣi along with his folloers came to the same forest. They took bath in teh river and worshipped Lord Hari, after which they took shelter of the shade of an āmakalī tree. There Viśvāmitra Ṛṣi began to describe the glories of the month of Kārttika as his disciples sat nearby to listen.

There happened to be a hunter roaming the forest at that time. He was particularly vicious, with a desire to kill not only animals but saintly people as well. However, when he came upon the great mahātmā, Viśvāmitra Ṛṣi and his disciples, he suddenly developed some intelligence. He offered his obeisances to Viśvāmitra Ṛṣi and asked, "What are you all doing here?" Viśvāmitra replied, "This Kārttika month is the best among all months. Any work done in this month grows like a huge banyan tree. If someone performs pious activities by taking an early morning bath, giving charity, worshipping and feeding brāhmaṇas in this month he achieves akṣaya or an imperishable result."

When Viśvāmitra thus described these glories of the month of Kārttika at the request of the hunter, the cursed son of Devaśarmā who was there in the form of a rat also heard this description. As soon as Viśvāmitra concluded his speech, the son of the brāhmaṇa who was hearing the glories of Kārttika-vrata immediately transformed, taking on a beautiful, celestial form. He offered his obeisances to Viśvāmitra Ṛṣi and explained his story, and then departed for the heavenly planets.

After witnessing this extraordinary event, both Viśv÷mitra and the hunter were very surprised. The hunter then observed Kārttika-vrata and went back to Vaikuṇṭha-loka. This narration clearly illustrates the importance of hearing the pastimes of Śrī Kṛṣṇa while sitting under the shade of an āmalakī tree during the month of Kārttika. By offering lamps to Śrī Hari while sitting under the āmalakī tree one will accumulate unlimited puṇya or pious results.
